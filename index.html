Rift Rampage

My first venture into the vast world of Virtual Reality gaming. It was made at CodeRed, Houston's Premier Hackathon. Made in Unity, Rift Rampage is a multiplayer first person shooter incorporating the Oculus Rift Virtual Reality headset and the LeapMotion Hand Controller.

The basic idea was that two players would face each other in a 3D virtual environment, where they would shoot at each other aiming to defeat the other. The players would hold out their hands over the LeapMotion device, and it would display their virtual hands on the screen. The player makes a tapping gesture with his thumb, and a bullet would shoot from his index finger.

Hence

Android app created in Android Studio Geofencing app that allows users to take self-guided tours or allow emergency services to notify people of events easily. A Geofence is a point on a map with a circle around it. When a user enters the circle, they get a notification that gives details of that particular spot.
Possible uses:
o Self-guided tours
o During a fire or car accident, a geofence can be placed to notify anyone nearby
o Find locations near a user, that they never knew existed

Senior Project

My senior projects that is being made in collaboration with George Erfesoglou
A multiplayer FPS game made in Unity and uses Photon networking for the online multiplayer.
For my part, I will be integrating the Oculus Rift and the LeapMotion devices for gameplay. We have devised a complex method of gameplay where the Oculus will only control the direction a player looks in. It will not control the direction of the weapon. That part will be handed off to the Leap, as will navigation

With your left hand, you will control your movement, and with your right, you will control the direction of your weapon.
You will effectively be able to "peak" around a corner, before bringing your gun around to shoot.
The game will also be cross-peripheral, so that those who do not own a Rift or a Leap can still play with a keyboard, mouse, monitor, or gamepad.
So far, the game is up and running. Oculus integration is the easy part. Will start working on Leap integration during Summer 2015.
